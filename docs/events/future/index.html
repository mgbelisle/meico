  • Becca

    A Story of Surrender

    April 16th, 2021

    Becca was diagnosed with cancer in 2017. During her journey, she relied on her family, her husband, her kids, her friends, and most of all, her champion, Jesus. Becca shared with us the many up's and down's of her experience, from the initial heartbreak of the diagnosis, telling her husband and her kids, surgery, snow and everything in between. Becca is an incredibly strong, God fearing woman who's resilience and strength has inspired many. Her husband and children speak to her grace and perseverance with adoration and awe and they share their experience in loving her through this difficult time. We are honored to listen and humbled to tell a part of her special and unique story.

  • Here to Listen

    Shunshine Personified

    July 25th - Aug 1st 2021

    Here to Listen 2021 is here! We are so excited to announce the amazing work we get to do this summer as we gather to dance, create, and listen. This year, we are honored to get to share the story of Keri Wilson, a fellow dancer and a ray of sunshine to all who knew her.

    Keri was born and raised in Omaha, Nebraska and began dancing at the age of 4. She quickly fell in love with the joy of movement and was at the studio everyday. Her parents, Kurt and Melinda Halvorson, supported Keri in her dancing and beyond. They were a constant presence and support in her life that inspired and encouraged others. During her college years at Texas Christian University, Keri became the president of the student dance organization, Calling in Action. After graduation, she joined Ingredients Dance Company in Dallas TX. and danced with the company for two years. Soon after this, while living in Florida, Keri met and married the love of her life, Corey Wilson. During this time, Keri began developing severe migraines. A new job for Corey took the newlyweds to Albuquerque, NM. Keri quickly found Dance Quest, the perfect studio for her to teach at and share her love for dance. The teachers and dancers there became her dance family in her final days. Unfortunately, Keri's migraines worsened. A doctor finally ordered an MRI and discovered that she had a very large brain tumor. After many tests, she had brain surgery in which they removed 99% of the tumor. Keri was then diagnosed with a rare form of stage 4 brain cancer called Glioblastoma. The average life expectancy for Glioblastoma is 15-16 months. There is currently no cure. Keri continued to teach dance until the end of that year, but by this time the tumor returned aggressively. She declined quickly throughout the winter of 2018. Keri passed away on April 18, 2018. Corey described Keri in her last moments "with joy on her face, knowing where she was going. She finished well." Her love for the Lord and the joy she spread are missed by so many. Those who had the privilege of knowing Keri will always remember her as, "Sunshine Personified."

    Come join us this summer as we dance this inspiring story of life, death, and the legacy of joy left behind.
